Reliable and trustworthy Signs You Need Squirrel Removal in South Whittier, CA

Noises in the Attic or Walls

One of the most common and unsettling signs of a squirrel infestation is hearing unexplained noises coming from your attic, walls, or ceiling, particularly during the daytime. Squirrels are diurnal, meaning they are most active during the day, especially in the early morning and late afternoon. These noises can range from scurrying and scratching to gnawing and chattering. The sounds are often distinct from those of rodents, which are typically more active at night. If you hear these noises consistently, it's highly likely that squirrels have taken up residence in your home.

The presence of squirrels in these enclosed spaces isn't merely an auditory annoyance; it can lead to significant structural damage. Their constant movement and gnawing can compromise the integrity of wooden beams, rafters, and other structural elements. Furthermore, their nesting activities can displace insulation, reducing your home's energy efficiency and potentially creating fire hazards if nests are built near electrical wiring. Recognizing these sounds early is a crucial step in protecting your property from further harm.

Visible Gnawing and Damage

Squirrels have a natural need to gnaw to keep their teeth sharp and prevent them from growing too long. This urge to chew can lead to extensive damage on various parts of your property. Look for visible signs of gnawing on electrical wires, wooden structures, siding, and fascia boards. They may also chew on pipes, compromising plumbing systems. Beyond structural elements, squirrels are notorious for damaging bird feeders, garden plants, and even outdoor furniture. Any unexplained damage to these items could be a strong indicator of squirrel activity on your property.

The consequences of this gnawing can be severe. Chewed electrical wires are a significant fire hazard and should be addressed immediately by a qualified professional. Damaged plumbing can lead to leaks and water damage, promoting mold growth. Beyond the functional issues, the aesthetic damage to your property from gnawing can decrease its curb appeal and value. Early detection and addressing gnawing signs are vital for protecting your investment and ensuring the safety of your home.

Finding Droppings and Urine Stains

The presence of animal waste in or around your home is a clear indicator of a pest problem. Squirrel droppings are typically small, pellet-shaped, and often found in concentrated areas where squirrels are nesting or feeding. Urine stains may appear as discolored patches on ceilings or walls, often accompanied by a strong, musky odor. These signs are not only unpleasant but also pose potential health risks to you and your family, as animal waste can carry various diseases and bacteria. Disturbing or cleaning up animal waste without proper protection can expose you to these hazards, making professional removal and sanitation essential.

Accumulated droppings and urine can also lead to structural damage over time, particularly to insulation and drywall. The unpleasant odor can permeate your home, making it uncomfortable to live in. Professional squirrel removal services include not only the removal of the animals but also the thorough cleaning and sanitation of affected areas, mitigating health risks and eliminating unpleasant odors. This comprehensive approach ensures that your home is not only free of squirrels but also safe and hygienic.

Reliable and trustworthy Understanding Common Squirrel Species in South Whittier, CA

South Whittier and the greater Southern California region are home to several species of squirrels, each with their own unique characteristics and behaviors. While our general squirrel removal techniques are effective for most species, understanding which type of squirrel you are dealing with can sometimes help in identifying their habits and preferred entry points. Being aware of the local squirrel population in areas like Downey can also contribute to a faster and more targeted resolution to your situation.

Eastern Fox Squirrel (Sciurus niger)

The Eastern Fox Squirrel is a large tree squirrel native to the eastern and central United States, but it has been introduced to California and is now common in urban and suburban areas like South Whittier. They are typically reddish-brown or gray with a bushy tail and can grow up to 27 inches in length, including their tail. Eastern Fox Squirrels are highly adaptable and thrive in environments with mature trees and readily available food sources. They are often seen foraging on the ground for nuts, seeds, and fruits, but they are also skilled climbers and can easily access attics and other overhead structures.

These squirrels are known for their tendency to build nests high up in trees, often in the crooks of branches. However, they will also seek shelter and build nests within structures, especially attics, chimneys, and wall voids. Their size and strength mean they can create larger entry points and cause more significant damage through gnawing compared to smaller squirrel species. Their adaptability makes them a common pest in residential areas, requiring effective and targeted wildlife control methods for removal. Understanding their nesting habits and preferred food sources can aid in both removal and preventative measures.

Western Gray Squirrel (Sciurus griseus)

The Western Gray Squirrel is a native tree squirrel of the western United States and is also found in parts of California, including wooded areas around South Whittier. They are larger than Eastern Fox Squirrels and have a distinctive bushy tail that is typically dark gray with white edges. Their fur is generally silver-gray, and they have a more slender build than the Eastern Fox Squirrel. Western Gray Squirrels are more dependent on conifer and oak forests for habitat and food, primarily feeding on acorns, pine nuts, and fungi. While they are primarily tree dwellers, they can still become a nuisance in urban areas, particularly properties adjacent to wooded areas.

Unlike the Eastern Fox Squirrel, Western Gray Squirrels are less prone to inhabiting human structures, preferring to build their nests in tree cavities or dreys (nests made of twigs and leaves) high up in trees. However, opportunities for shelter, such as uncapped chimneys or damaged attics near wooded areas, can still attract them. They are generally shyer than Eastern Fox Squirrels but are still capable of causing damage if they gain access to a building. Identifying whether you have Western Gray Squirrels can inform the strategy for removal and exclusion, focusing on access points most likely to be exploited by this species. Their presence often indicates your property is close to their natural habitat.

Ground Squirrels (various species)

While our primary focus is on tree squirrels entering structures, it's worth noting the presence of ground squirrels in South Whittier, CA. Several species of ground squirrels inhabit California, including the California Ground Squirrel (Otospermophilus beecheyi). These squirrels are burrowing animals that create complex tunnel systems underground, often found in yards, gardens, and parks. They are typically yellowish-tan or gray with speckled fur and have a shorter, less bushy tail compared to tree squirrels. Ground squirrels primarily feed on grasses, seeds, and roots and can cause damage to landscaping and lawns through their burrowing activities.

Although ground squirrels rarely enter human structures, their burrows can undermine foundations and create tripping hazards. Their presence on your property can also attract predators. While our rodent control services can address ground squirrel issues, our squirrel removal service specifically targets the tree squirrels that are typically found within attics and walls. Understanding the distinction between ground squirrels and tree squirrels is important for choosing the appropriate pest control service to address your specific problem. If you are dealing with burrowing animals causing damage to your yard, our expert team can assist with identifying the culprit and implementing effective solutions tailored to ground squirrel control.

Potential Fire Hazards

One of the most serious risks associated with squirrel infestations is the potential for fire. Squirrels have a natural instinct to gnaw, and unfortunately, electrical wiring is often a target. Chewing on wires can strip away the insulation, exposing live wires, which can lead to short circuits and potentially ignite a fire. Attics and wall voids are common nesting areas for squirrels and are also where a significant amount of electrical wiring is located, increasing the risk. This is a critical safety concern that should never be overlooked.

Repairing damaged electrical wiring caused by squirrels requires the expertise of a qualified electrician after the squirrels have been safely removed. Our squirrel removal service includes a thorough inspection for any visible signs of chewed wiring, and we will advise you on the necessary steps to address any potential fire hazards. Protecting your property from the risk of fire is a primary reason to seek professional squirrel removal services as soon as you suspect an infestation. Squirrels may also damage roofing materials, siding, and fascia boards to gain entry, creating openings for water damage and other pests to enter.
